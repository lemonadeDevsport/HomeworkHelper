What is Artificial Intelligence?

    Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the simulation of human intelligence in machines that are programmed to think, learn, and make decisions. In other words, AI allows computers and software to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as understanding language, recognizing patterns, solving problems, and even making decisions based on data. AI can be broken down into two main types:

  • Narrow AI:

    This is AI that is designed to perform a specific task, like facial recognition, language translation, or playing chess. Most AI systems today are narrow AI.

  • General AI

    This is the more advanced form of AI that can perform any intellectual task that a human can do. It is still a concept and not yet a reality.

    • AI works through algorithms and machine learning, where the system is trained on data to recognize patterns and improve over time without being explicitly programmed for every task.

Ethical AI Use

    StudyTrack's Next Gen AI Model plays a big role in improving the study experience for students. However, it’s essential to ensure that the AI is used ethically to create a fair, secure, and responsible environment. Here are our key principles that we are contracted to follow to ensure ethical AI use:

  • Advance, Not Replace

    The model enhances a student's learning experience, not do the work for them. The goal is to help students learn and grow, not simply provide answers.
    How it applies:
    Assist with practice problems, offer hints, or explain concepts, but it should not provide answers outright without encouraging students to think critically. For example, if a student asks for help with an essay, the AI might suggest areas for improvement or offer resources, but it should not write the essay for them. The focus should always be on supporting independent learning and improving skills.

  • Secured Data

    The personal data of students is to always be protected and used responsibly. This AI system should not misuse or improperly share sensitive information.
    How it applies:
    When students interact with StudyTrack, their data—such as their name, assignments, and performance—are kept private and secure. StudyTrack operates under strict data privacy standards, including GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation), ensuring that any data used for personalization or feedback is anonymized or encrypted to prevent unauthorized access.

  • Transparency

    Students have access to the certain background schematics in order to understand how the AI works and how it makes decisions, especially when providing feedback or answers.
    How it applies:
    If a student asks for help on a math problem or receives a recommendation on how to improve their writing, the AI is trained to clearly explain why it gave that answer or suggestion. This transparency helps build trust and ensures students understand the reasoning behind the AI-assisted decisions.
